# Multi-Voice Communication Guide

> This guide defines Your communication voice across three distinct modes: Casual, Professional, and Formal. Each mode serves different contexts in [your service type] consulting work.

---

## Core Communication Principles

### Clarity First
- **What it means**: Clear, direct communication that gets to the point
- **In practice**: Lead with the main point; use simple language; avoid jargon unless necessary
- **Why**: Supports executive function; reduces cognitive load; enables action

### Actionability
- **What it means**: Every communication should make next steps clear
- **In practice**: End with concrete actions, decisions, or questions; provide clear recommendations
- **Why**: Helps clients move forward; reduces decision paralysis; drives outcomes

### Adaptive Voice
- **What it means**: Match communication style to context and audience
- **In practice**: Casual for internal work, Professional for clients, Formal for official contexts
- **Why**: Builds rapport appropriately; maintains professionalism; meets expectations

---

## The Three Communication Modes

### Casual Mode
**Use for**: Internal notes, Slack messages, quick emails, thinking-out-loud documents, personal task lists

**Characteristics**:
- Conversational, friendly tone
- Contractions are fine (it's, you're, we'll)
- Incomplete sentences acceptable if meaning is clear
- Bullet points and quick lists
- Emoji acceptable for tone/context ✅ ⚠️ 💡

**Example contexts**:
- Internal project notes
- Slack channel updates
- Personal reminders and todos
- Quick check-in messages
- Brainstorming docs

### Professional Mode
**Use for**: Client emails, proposals, meeting summaries, standard business communication, consulting recommendations

**Characteristics**:
- Clear, polished, but approachable
- Complete sentences with proper grammar
- Structured but not stiff
- Active voice preferred
- Balance between friendly and professional

**Example contexts**:
- Client email communications
- Meeting follow-ups and summaries
- Consulting proposals and SOWs
- Project status updates
- Client-facing documentation

### Formal Mode
**Use for**: RFP responses, executive presentations, official documentation, contracts, compliance materials

**Characteristics**:
- Precise, polished language
- Full sentences with careful structure
- Industry-standard terminology
- Third-person acceptable where appropriate
- Zero tolerance for typos or errors

**Example contexts**:
- RFP and tender responses
- Executive board presentations
- Official reports and assessments
- Compliance documentation
- Contract proposals

## Mode-Specific Guidelines

### Casual Mode - Quick Reference

**Sentence Structure**:
- Short, punchy sentences
- Fragments OK if clear
- Lists and bullets
- Use "I think" and "maybe" freely

**Opening Lines**:
- "Quick thought on..."
- "Heads up -"
- "Just noticed..."
- "FYI:"

**Closing Patterns**:
- "LMK if questions"
- "Thoughts?"
- "Does this make sense?"
- No formal sign-off needed

### Professional Mode - Quick Reference

**Sentence Structure**:
- Clear, complete sentences
- One idea per paragraph
- Use headers for structure
- Active voice: "I recommend" not "It is recommended"

**Opening Lines**:
- "Following up on our discussion about..."
- "I wanted to share my thoughts on..."
- "Based on our conversation, here are the next steps..."
- "Thank you for the opportunity to..."

**Closing Patterns**:
- "Please let me know if you have any questions."
- "I'm happy to discuss this further."
- "Looking forward to your feedback."
- "Next steps: [clear action items]"

### Formal Mode - Quick Reference

**Sentence Structure**:
- Precise, well-structured sentences
- Clear topic sentences
- Logical flow and transitions
- Formal connectors (however, therefore, consequently)

**Opening Lines**:
- "This document outlines..."
- "The following assessment provides..."
- "Based on comprehensive analysis..."
- "Executive Summary: [key findings]"

**Closing Patterns**:
- "We recommend the following course of action..."
- "This proposal outlines a comprehensive approach to..."
- "For additional information, please contact..."
- "Respectfully submitted,"

## [Field]-Specific Vocabulary

### Core Terms
- **[Your Field] (CX)**: The complete customer journey and all touchpoints
- **Support Operations**: Day-to-day support delivery, metrics, processes
- **Customer Success**: Proactive relationship management and value realization
- **Service Level Agreement (SLA)**: Response and resolution time commitments
- **Customer Health Score**: Metric combining engagement, satisfaction, product usage
- **Escalation Path**: Process for handling complex issues up the chain
- **Knowledge Base**: Self-service documentation and resources
- **Ticket Volume**: Number of support requests received
- **First Response Time (FRT)**: Time from ticket creation to first response
- **Resolution Time**: Time from ticket creation to resolution
- **CSAT**: Customer Satisfaction score
- **NPS**: Net Promoter Score

### Phrases to Use
- "Based on my experience with similar companies..."
- "Industry best practice suggests..."
- "The data shows..."
- "This aligns with our goal of..."
- "Here's what I recommend..."
- "Let me break this down..."

### Phrases to Avoid
- Overpromising: "This will definitely..." → "This approach should..."
- Hedging excessively: "Maybe we could possibly..." → "I recommend..."
- Vague metrics: "Much better" → "20% improvement in CSAT"
- Jargon without context: Define terms first time they appear

## Writing Process Integration

### Mode Selection Decision Tree
1. **Who's the audience?**
   - Just me → Casual
   - Client/colleague → Professional
   - Executive/official → Formal

2. **What's the purpose?**
   - Quick note/reminder → Casual
   - Business communication → Professional
   - Official record/proposal → Formal

3. **What's the context?**
   - Internal thinking → Casual
   - Standard client work → Professional
   - High-stakes or compliance → Formal

### Quality Checks by Mode

**Casual Mode**:
- ✅ Is the meaning clear?
- ✅ Would I understand this later?
- ✅ Are action items obvious?

**Professional Mode**:
- ✅ Is it free of typos and grammar errors?
- ✅ Is the tone appropriate for the relationship?
- ✅ Are next steps crystal clear?
- ✅ Would I be comfortable if this were forwarded?

**Formal Mode**:
- ✅ Is every fact verified and accurate?
- ✅ Is the structure logical and complete?
- ✅ Is the language precise and professional?
- ✅ Does it represent my best work?

## Handling Writer's Block

### When Stuck (Executive Function Support)

**Start with bullets**:
- Just list the main points
- Don't worry about sentences yet
- Get ideas out of your head
- Organize later

**Use sentence starters**:
- "The main point is..."
- "Here's what I'm thinking..."
- "The key challenge is..."
- "I recommend..."

**Lower the bar**:
- Casual mode for first draft
- Upgrade to Professional/Formal after
- Something is better than nothing
- AI can help polish later

**Use templates**:
- Copy structure from above
- Fill in your content
- Templates remove the blank page
- Adapt as needed
