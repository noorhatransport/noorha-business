## Purpose

This file catalogs effective communication examples across three voice modes:
- **Casual**: Internal notes, quick messages
- **Professional**: Client communication, business writing
- **Formal**: Official documents, proposals, assessments

Examples serve as reference patterns for future communication.

### Internal Planning Note

#### Example: Client Transition Planning
```
# Client X Transition Notes

Main thing: They're losing their [Field] lead, need fractional support during search.

Opportunity:
- 3-month engagement, 15 hrs/week
- Interim leadership + recruiting support
- Could extend if hire takes longer

Scope:
- Run weekly leadership meetings
- Review support metrics
- Interview [Field] candidates
- Knowledge transfer to new hire

Pricing thought: $8K/month (15 hrs @ $133/hr effective)

Next actions:
- [ ] Draft proposal by Friday
- [ ] Send pricing options
- [ ] Schedule kickoff call

Questions:
- Do they want me involved in final hire decision?
- What's their timeline for filling the role?
```

**Why This Works**:
- Quick, clear capture of opportunity
- Casual tone for internal thinking
- Structured enough to be actionable
- Questions captured for follow-up

### [Field] Assessment Report - Formal Deliverable

#### Example: Support Operations Health Check
```
# [Your Field] Assessment: TechStart Inc.

## Executive Summary

**Assessment Period**: November 1-30, 2024
**Methodology**: Ticket analysis (300 tickets), team interviews (5 people), customer survey (50 responses), metrics benchmarking

### Key Findings
1. **Response time is industry-leading** (1.8 hour FRT vs 3-4 hour benchmark)
2. **Resolution time significantly exceeds benchmark** (4.5 days vs 1.5 day industry average)
3. **Team capacity at 95%** - no room for volume growth without adding headcount
4. **Knowledge documentation gap** - 80% of knowledge is undocumented tribal knowledge
5. **Customer satisfaction declining** as complexity and volume increase (3.6 CSAT, down from 4.2)

### Priority Recommendations
1. **Immediate**: Implement escalation process to reduce resolution time
2. **Short-term**: Hire 2 additional L1 support specialists
3. **Medium-term**: Develop comprehensive knowledge base

---

## Current State Analysis

### Strengths
- **Fast initial response**: 1.8 hour average FRT (top quartile)
- **High team engagement**: Support team morale and commitment is strong
- **Customer relationship quality**: Personal touch valued by customers
- **Product knowledge**: Deep understanding of platform

### Challenges
- **Resolution bottleneck**: Complex issues getting stuck, taking 4+ days
- **Capacity constraint**: Team at maximum capacity with no buffer
- **Knowledge silos**: Information trapped in individual team members
- **Scaling risk**: Current model won't support 2x customer growth
- **Burnout risk**: Team working at unsustainable pace

### Metrics Snapshot

| Metric | Current | Industry Benchmark | Gap |
|--------|---------|-------------------|-----|
| CSAT | 3.6/5.0 | 4.2/5.0 | -0.6 |
| NPS | +15 | +30 to +40 | -15 to -25 |
| First Response Time | 1.8 hours | 3-4 hours | ✅ Leading |
| Resolution Time | 4.5 days | 1.5 days | ⚠️ 3 days slower |
| Ticket Volume | 180/month | Varies | Growing 15%/month |
| Team Utilization | 95% | 80% sustainable | ⚠️ Unsustainable |

---

## Detailed Findings

### Finding 1: No Formal Escalation Process

**Impact**: HIGH
**Current State**: Complex issues circulate among team via Slack until someone has time to tackle them. No SLA for escalated issues. Average escalated ticket takes 6.2 days to resolve.
**Why This Matters**: 30% of tickets require escalation. These long-resolution-time tickets are driving CSAT decline and team frustration.
**Recommendation**: Implement formal escalation matrix with L1→L2→L3 paths and SLA commitments at each level.

### Finding 2: Knowledge Base Doesn't Exist

**Impact**: HIGH
**Current State**: No self-service resources. Every question requires ticket. Support team answering same questions repeatedly (estimate 40% of tickets are repeat questions).
**Why This Matters**: Missing opportunity to deflect 30-40% of tickets. Customers prefer self-service for simple questions.
**Recommendation**: Develop initial knowledge base with 25-30 articles covering most common questions. Target: Reduce ticket volume by 25% within 60 days.
```

## Voice Mode Comparison

### Same Message, Three Modes

**Scenario**: Need to communicate support metrics declining

**Casual (Internal Note)**:
```
FYI - Support metrics trending down:
- CSAT: 3.8 (was 4.2 last month)
- Resolution time: 4.5 days (was 3.2)

Main issue looks like team capacity - they're underwater with 15% volume increase.

Need to talk about hiring timeline.
```

**Professional (Client Email)**:
```
Hi Sarah,

Quick heads-up on support metrics from this month.

We're seeing some concerning trends:
- CSAT dropped from 4.2 to 3.8
- Resolution time increased from 3.2 to 4.5 days
- Ticket volume up 15% month-over-month

The root cause appears to be capacity - the team is at maximum utilization with the increased volume.

I recommend we accelerate the hiring timeline we discussed. The metrics will likely continue declining without additional support capacity.

Can we schedule 30 minutes this week to discuss hiring priorities?

Best,
Ash
```

**Formal (Board Report)**:
```
## [Your Field] Metrics Update

### Executive Summary
Support operations metrics declined in November 2024, driven primarily by capacity constraints as ticket volume increased 15% month-over-month.

### Key Metrics
- Customer Satisfaction (CSAT): 3.8 (-0.4 from October)
- Average Resolution Time: 4.5 days (+1.3 days from October)
- Monthly Ticket Volume: 207 (+15% from October)
- Team Utilization: 95% (above sustainable threshold of 80%)

### Root Cause Analysis
The support team is operating at maximum capacity. The 15% increase in ticket volume has exceeded available team hours, resulting in longer queue times and declining satisfaction scores.

### Recommended Action
Accelerate planned hiring to add 2 L1 Support Specialists by end of Q1 2025. This will reduce team utilization to sustainable levels and improve response consistency.

### Impact if Unaddressed
Continued metric decline likely as volume grows. Risk of team burnout and increased customer churn.
```
