## Purpose

This guide provides vocabulary reference for the three communication modes:
- **Casual**: Internal notes, quick communications

## Words to Use Confidently

### Action Words
- **Implement** - Execute a plan or strategy
- **Optimize** - Make more efficient or effective
- **Scale** - Grow sustainably
- **Streamline** - Remove unnecessary complexity
- **Enhance** - Improve existing functionality
- **Establish** - Create something new and foundational
- **Develop** - Build capability over time
- **Deploy** - Put into production/operation

### Qualification Words
- **Typically** - Usually happens this way
- **Generally** - In most cases
- **Often** - Frequently but not always
- **Commonly** - Standard practice
- **Usually** - Most of the time
- **Likely** - Probable outcome

### Emphasis Words
- **Critical** - Must be addressed
- **Essential** - Necessary for success
- **Key** - Important element
- **Significant** - Meaningful impact
- **Substantial** - Large impact
- **Notable** - Worth mentioning

## Words to Avoid or Clarify

### Overpromising Words
❌ **Don't use without qualification**:
- "Definitely" → "This approach should..."
- "Guaranteed" → "Expected to..."
- "Always" → "Typically" or "In most cases"
- "Never" → "Rarely" or "Uncommon"
- "Revolutionary" → "Significant improvement"
- "Transform" → "Improve" or "Enhance"

### Vague Terms
❌ **Replace with specifics**:
- "Much better" → "20% improvement in CSAT"
- "More efficient" → "Reduced resolution time by 2 days"
- "Significant increase" → "45% increase in self-service rate"
- "Soon" → "By end of Q1" or "Within 2 weeks"
- "Quickly" → "Within 48 hours"

### Jargon Without Context
❌ **Define first time used in formal documents**:
- "Health score" (explain calculation)
- "Tier 1" (explain support model)
- "SLA" (spell out: Service Level Agreement)
- "Customer success motion" (explain approach)

## Phrases for Common Situations

### Delivering Bad News
**Professional Mode**:
- "Here's what we're seeing..."
- "The challenge we're facing is..."
- "Unfortunately, the data shows..."
- "We need to address..."

**What to avoid**:
- Burying the lead
- Excessive hedging
- Blame language

### Asking for Decisions
**Professional Mode**:
- "I need your input on..."
- "Please confirm your preference for..."
- "Which option aligns better with your goals?"
- "By [date], we need to decide..."

**Formal Mode**:
- "Approval is requested for..."
- "Leadership decision required regarding..."
- "Please advise on the following..."

### Presenting Options
**Framework**:
1. "Here are three options..."
2. "Option A: [brief description]
   - Pros: [list]
   - Cons: [list]"
3. "I recommend Option [X] because..."

### Declining Requests Professionally
**Templates**:
- "I appreciate you thinking of me, but I'm not able to take this on given [reason]."
- "This is outside my area of focus. I recommend [alternative]."
- "My current capacity doesn't allow for this. Can we revisit in [timeframe]?"

## Data & Numbers

### How to Present Numbers

**Percentages**:
- Use % symbol: "15% improvement"
- Round appropriately: "approximately 15%" for estimates

**Large Numbers**:
- Use comma separators: "10,000 tickets"
- Use K/M for context: "10K monthly tickets"

**Time Periods**:
- Be specific: "Q1 2025" not "early next year"
- Use consistent format: "January 15, 2025" or "2025-01-15"

**Ranges**:
- Use clear notation: "10-20 hours/week"
- Provide context: "$5-15K/month (depends on scope)"

### Introducing Data

**Professional Mode**:
- "The data shows..."
- "Based on [source], we see..."
- "Analysis reveals..."
- "Metrics indicate..."

**Formal Mode**:
- "Data analysis demonstrates..."
- "Quantitative assessment reveals..."
- "Empirical evidence suggests..."

## Communication Principles Applied

### Clarity
✅ **Use**: Short sentences, active voice, specific terms
❌ **Avoid**: Nested clauses, passive voice, vague language

### Actionability
✅ **Use**: Clear next steps, assigned owners, due dates
❌ **Avoid**: "We should consider..." without specifics

### Professionalism
✅ **Use**: Respectful tone, appropriate formality
❌ **Avoid**: Overly casual in formal contexts, stuffiness in casual contexts
