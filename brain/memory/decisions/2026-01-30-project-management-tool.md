# Decision: Project Management Tool - Linear (Deferred to March)

**Date**: January 30, 2026
**Decision Maker(s)**: Zulfan Hameed
**Status**: Approved - Implementation deferred to March 2026
**Category**: Operations, Tools

---

## Context

With Muhammad Ismail starting February 1 as founding member, need to decide on project management approach for coordinating work, tracking progress, and managing daily standups.

**February Priorities**:
- Website Revamp (P0)
- GMB Optimization (P0)
- Brand Identity (P0)

**Team Size**: 2 (Zulfan + Muhammad)

**Timeline Pressure**: 3 months to hit 40 bookings/month target (by May 1)

---

## Decision

**Tool Selected**: Linear (linear.app)

**Implementation Timeline**: March 2026 (not February)

**February Approach**: Speed over process
- Daily standups (verbal/WhatsApp coordination)
- No formal task tracking
- Direct communication, fast iteration
- Focus 100% on execution, not overhead

**March Transition**: Introduce Linear for more structured tracking once foundation built

---

## Rationale

### Why Linear

**Technical Fit**:
- Muhammad is technical (will appreciate developer-focused tool)
- Clean, fast interface (doesn't slow down work)
- AI-powered features (aligns with AI-native vision)
- Strong keyboard shortcuts and workflows

**Scalability**:
- Free tier sufficient to start
- Scales well as team grows beyond 2 people
- Good API for future integrations (potential MCP connection)
- Used by modern, fast-moving teams

**Comparison to Alternatives**:
- **vs Height**: Linear more established, better for technical teams
- **vs Notion**: Linear faster for task management, less setup overhead
- **vs Jira**: Linear much cleaner, less enterprise bloat
- **vs ClickUp**: Linear simpler, less feature creep

### Why Defer to March

**February = Sprint Mode**:
Muhammad needs to move at maximum velocity:
- Build website from scratch
- Design brand identity
- Optimize GMB completely
- Learn Noorha's business

Adding PM tool overhead in Month 1 would slow him down.

**Founding Team Reality**:
With just 2 people in daily contact:
- Coordination is simple (daily standups)
- Tasks are obvious (Feb projects documented in memory)
- Overhead doesn't pay off yet
- Communication > formal tracking

**March Makes Sense**:
By March:
- Foundation built (website, GMB, brand live)
- Muhammad familiar with Noorha
- Moving into content strategy (more tasks, more coordination)
- Tracking becomes valuable (blog posts, off-page SEO, campaigns)
- Team may start growing (more people = more coordination need)

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Lose Track of Tasks in February
**Likelihood**: Low (only 3 projects, 2 people, daily standups)
**Impact**: Medium

**Mitigation**:
- Projects well-documented in `memory/projects/active/`
- Daily standups catch anything slipping
- Only 1 month before Linear introduced

### Risk 2: Hard to Transition to Linear in March
**Likelihood**: Low (Linear is easy to learn)
**Impact**: Low

**Mitigation**:
- Muhammad is technical, learns tools quickly
- Linear has great onboarding
- Start simple, expand usage gradually

### Risk 3: February Chaos Without Structure
**Likelihood**: Very Low
**Impact**: Medium

**Mitigation**:
- This is exactly how founding teams should work (fast, minimal process)
- Zulfan and Muhammad both experienced professionals
- Daily standups provide structure
- Clear February priorities (Brand → Website → GMB)

## Lessons

### For Future Tool Decisions

**Don't introduce overhead prematurely**:
- Tools should solve problems, not create them
- Process for process sake slows teams down
- Founding teams need speed, not structure

**Timing matters**:
- Month 1 = Sprint and build foundation
- Month 2+ = Add structure as complexity grows
- Right tool at wrong time = wrong decision

**Keep it simple**:
- Two people don't need complex PM systems
- Daily standups > task tracking at this stage
- Ship first, systematize later
