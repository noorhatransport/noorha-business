## How to Use This Log

### When to Document a Decision

**Always document**:
- Hiring decisions
- Major strategy pivots
- Significant financial commitments
- Partnership or equity grants
- New service offerings or market entry
- Technology/tool selections that affect workflow
- Changes to compensation or business model

**Optional to document**:
- Minor tactical adjustments
- Routine operational choices
- Decisions that are easily reversible

### Decision Template

Each decision file should contain:

```markdown
# Decision: [Title]

**Date**: [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Decision Maker(s)**: [Who made this decision]
**Status**: [Proposed | Approved | Executed | Reversed]

---

## Context

[What situation led to this decision? What problem were you solving?]

## Options Considered

### Option 1: [Name]
**Pros**:
- [Pro 1]
- [Pro 2]

**Cons**:
- [Con 1]
- [Con 2]

### Option 2: [Name]
[Same format]

## Decision

[What was decided and why]

## Rationale

[Key factors that led to this choice]

## Expected Outcomes

- [Outcome 1]
- [Outcome 2]

## Success Criteria

How will we know if this decision was correct?
- [Metric 1]
- [Metric 2]

## Review Date

[When will we revisit this decision?]

## Related Decisions

- [Link to related decision files]
```

## Learning from Decisions

### Monthly Review
During monthly retrospectives:
1. Review decisions made this month
2. Assess early outcomes
3. Extract learnings
4. Adjust course if needed

### Pattern Extraction
Use `/learn` to identify:
- What types of decisions consistently work well
- What factors lead to good outcomes
- Where we tend to over/under-think
- How to improve decision-making process
