# Positioning

> How you position yourself in the market and the unique angle that makes you stand out.

---

## What Goes Here

Positioning is **your strategic market identity** - how you define your space, who you're for, what problem you solve, and why you're uniquely qualified to solve it. This shapes every piece of content, every sales conversation, and every client engagement.

### Include
- ✅ Your category or market position
- ✅ Who you serve (and who you don't)
- ✅ The specific problem you solve
- ✅ Your unique approach or methodology
- ✅ What makes you different from alternatives
- ✅ Your "unfair advantages"
- ✅ How you want to be perceived

### Don't Include
- ❌ Generic statements anyone could claim
- ❌ Positioning you aspire to but haven't earned
- ❌ Multiple conflicting positions
- ❌ Positioning that doesn't match your actual work

---

## Positioning Types

### Category Positioning
**Purpose**: What space you own or want to own
**Example**: "The content strategist for B2B SaaS founders"

### Problem Positioning
**Purpose**: The specific problem you solve
**Example**: "I help founders who can't articulate why they're different"

### Methodology Positioning
**Purpose**: Your unique approach or framework
**Example**: "Position-first content strategy"

### Audience Positioning
**Purpose**: Who you exclusively serve
**Example**: "Only working with technical founders in fintech"
