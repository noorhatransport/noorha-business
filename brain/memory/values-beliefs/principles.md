## Purpose

## Core Consulting Principles

### 1. Understand Before Recommending

> "Never prescribe without diagnosis."

**The Principle**: Always start with assessment and data collection before making recommendations.

**Why This Matters**: Protects against cookie-cutter solutions; ensures recommendations fit actual context

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Ticket analysis, team interviews, metrics review, customer feedback
- **Don't**: Apply "best practices" without understanding current state
- **Rule**: Spend 20% of engagement on assessment minimum

**When Tested**: Client wants recommendations immediately without assessment
**How to Stay True**: "I need to understand your specific context before I can recommend what will work for you. Let me start with some data collection."

---

### 2. Build Capability, Don't Create Dependency

> "The best consulting makes itself obsolete."

**The Principle**: Every engagement should leave the client stronger and more capable.

**Why This Matters**: Sustainable business comes from client success, not dependency; clients value capability building

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Documentation, training, knowledge transfer, coaching
- **Don't**: Keep knowledge to yourself, create complex systems only you understand
- **Rule**: Every deliverable includes "how to sustain this" section

**When Tested**: Opportunity to extend engagement by not documenting fully
**How to Stay True**: "Part of my value is ensuring you can maintain this after I'm gone. Here's the documentation."

---

### 3. Measure Everything That Matters

> "If you're not measuring it, you're guessing."

**The Principle**: Establish baselines, define metrics, track outcomes for all significant work.

**Why This Matters**: Data shows what's actually working; enables continuous improvement; builds credibility

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Baseline metrics before changes, track during, measure outcomes after
- **Don't**: Make changes without measurement, use anecdotes as proof
- **Rule**: Every recommendation includes success metrics

**When Tested**: Client wants to skip baseline measurement to "move faster"
**How to Stay True**: "We need baseline data to know if our changes actually work. This pays off in confidence later."

---

### 4. Start Simple, Iterate Based on Data

> "Perfect is the enemy of shipped."

**The Principle**: Begin with simplest viable solution, then improve based on evidence.

**Why This Matters**: Faster to value; lower risk; enables learning; avoids over-engineering

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: MVP approach, quick wins, then optimization
- **Don't**: Design perfect solution upfront, boil the ocean, over-complicate
- **Rule**: Phase work - start with highest impact, simplest execution

**When Tested**: Temptation to design comprehensive solution before validating
**How to Stay True**: "Let's start with the 20% that will drive 80% of results, measure, then expand."

---

### 5. Transparent Communication Always

> "Clarity is kindness."

**The Principle**: Direct, honest communication even when uncomfortable.

**Why This Matters**: Trust requires transparency; problems hidden don't get solved; clarity enables action

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Share concerns early, be direct about challenges, admit uncertainty
- **Don't**: Sugarcoat problems, avoid difficult conversations, hide concerns
- **Rule**: If I'm worried about something, client needs to know

**When Tested**: Bad news that might upset client
**How to Stay True**: "I need to share something concerning I'm seeing in the data..."

---

### 6. Protect Sustainable Pace

> "Marathon, not sprint."

**The Principle**: Maintain sustainable workload for both yourself and client teams.

**Why This Matters**: Burnout helps no one; sustainable pace enables quality; protects long-term relationships

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Flag over-utilization, build buffer capacity, set realistic timelines
- **Don't**: Accept unrealistic deadlines, push teams beyond sustainable capacity
- **Rule**: Team utilization target is 70-80%, not 95%+

**When Tested**: Client wants faster delivery than sustainable
**How to Stay True**: "I can deliver faster, but quality will suffer and the team will burn out. Here's a sustainable timeline."

---

### 7. Challenge Conventional Wisdom

> "Best practice for whom?"

**The Principle**: Question whether "best practices" actually fit this client's context.

**Why This Matters**: Cookie-cutter solutions often fail; context matters; differentiation comes from customization

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Ask "why" about current approaches, test assumptions, adapt practices to context
- **Don't**: Blindly apply industry standards, assume what works elsewhere will work here
- **Rule**: Every "best practice" recommendation includes "why it fits your context"

**When Tested**: Client expects standard playbook application
**How to Stay True**: "That's the standard approach, but given your context, I think we should adapt it because..."

---

### 8. Document Decisions and Rationale

> "Future you will thank present you."

**The Principle**: Capture why decisions were made, not just what was decided.

**Why This Matters**: Prevents repeating past mistakes; enables learning; helps future team members understand context

**In Practice**:
- **Do**: Decision memos with options considered and rationale
- **Don't**: Make decisions without recording reasoning
- **Rule**: Significant decisions get documented with context

**When Tested**: Moving fast and tempted to skip documentation
**How to Stay True**: "Five minutes now saves hours of confusion later."

## When Principles Conflict

### Speed vs Quality
**Conflict**: Client wants fast results; quality takes time
**Resolution**: Start simple and iterate (Principle 4) while being transparent about trade-offs (Principle 5)
**Priority**: Quality of outcome > speed, but deliver value incrementally

### Revenue vs Client Best Interest
**Conflict**: Opportunity to expand scope; client might not need it
**Resolution**: Client success over revenue (Principle 2 + Value 1)
**Priority**: Long-term relationship > short-term revenue

### Certainty vs Transparency
**Conflict**: Don't have all answers; client expects expertise
**Resolution**: Transparent about uncertainty (Principle 5); use data to reduce ambiguity (Principle 3)
**Priority**: Honesty > appearing to know everything

## Boundaries & Standards

### I Will Not
- Take on engagements without proper assessment time
- Make recommendations unsupported by data
- Create solutions that require unsustainable effort
- Hide concerns or sugarcoat problems
- Apply "best practices" without contextual adaptation
- Build dependency instead of capability
- Accept timelines that compromise quality
- Skip documentation of significant decisions

### I Will Always
- Start with data and assessment
- Challenge assumptions (including my own)
- Build for sustainability
- Communicate transparently
- Document systematically
- Transfer knowledge proactively
- Measure outcomes
- Maintain sustainable pace
