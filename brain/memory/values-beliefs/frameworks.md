## Support Operations Frameworks

### Tiered Support Model Framework
**Purpose**: Scale support operations efficiently while maintaining quality

**Core Components**:
- **Tier 1 (L1)**: First-line support - Basic questions, account issues, documentation
  - Target: Handle 60-70% of all tickets
  - Skills: Product knowledge, customer service, basic troubleshooting
  - Metrics: FRT under 2 hours, 70% resolution rate

- **Tier 2 (L2)**: Technical support - Complex troubleshooting, bug triage
  - Target: Handle 25-30% of tickets
  - Skills: Deep technical knowledge, advanced troubleshooting, customer communication
  - Metrics: Resolution rate 80%+, escalation clarity

- **Tier 3 (L3)**: Engineering escalation - Complex bugs, architecture questions
  - Target: Handle <10% of tickets
  - Skills: Engineering expertise, system architecture knowledge
  - Metrics: Minimize engineering support time

**When to Apply**:
- Company growing from 100 to 500+ customers
- All-hands support model breaking down
- Engineering spending >15% time on support
- Response times degrading due to volume

**Benefits**:
- Scalable structure (add more L1 as volume grows)
- Clear career progression path
- Protects engineering capacity
- Enables specialization and expertise development

### Customer Health Scoring Framework
**Purpose**: Proactive identification of at-risk customers

**Core Components**:
- **Product Usage** (40% weight): Login frequency, feature adoption, active users
- **Support Engagement** (30% weight): Ticket volume, severity, sentiment
- **Relationship Health** (30% weight): QBR attendance, response rates, renewal conversations

**Health Score Bands**:
- 80-100: Healthy (monitor quarterly)
- 60-79: At risk (proactive outreach)
- 40-59: Critical (immediate intervention)
- 0-39: Churning (executive escalation)

**When to Apply**:
- Customer base >50 customers (manual monitoring no longer scales)
- Churn becoming revenue risk
- Need proactive customer success approach

**Applications**:
- Monthly health score review with client
- Trigger proactive outreach for declining scores
- Prioritize customer success resources
- Early warning system for renewals

### Support Metrics Hierarchy Framework
**Purpose**: Focus on metrics that actually drive outcomes

**Core Metrics (The "Big 3")**:
1. **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)**: Outcome metric - are customers happy?
2. **First Response Time (FRT)**: Leading indicator - how fast do we respond?
3. **Resolution Time**: Efficiency metric - how quickly do we solve problems?

**Supporting Metrics**:
- Ticket Volume (capacity planning)
- Resolution Rate (quality indicator)
- Escalation Rate (process efficiency)
- Self-Service Rate (efficiency multiplier)

**Diagnostic Metrics**:
- Team utilization (burnout indicator)
- Re-open rate (quality check)
- Customer effort score (friction indicator)

**When to Apply**: Setting up metrics for first time or auditing existing metrics

**Key Principle**: Start with the Big 3, add supporting metrics only when actionable

### Support Process Maturity Model
**Purpose**: Assess current state and plan evolution path

**Level 1: Ad-hoc**
- Characteristics: All-hands support, no documentation, reactive only
- Typical Stage: 0-50 customers
- Indicator: "Whoever sees the email answers it"

**Level 2: Basic Process**
- Characteristics: Designated support people, basic ticketing, some documentation
- Typical Stage: 50-200 customers
- Indicator: "We have a support team but processes are informal"

**Level 3: Defined Process**
- Characteristics: Tiered support, documented workflows, SLA commitments, knowledge base
- Typical Stage: 200-500 customers
- Indicator: "We have standard operating procedures"

**Level 4: Optimized**
- Characteristics: Proactive customer success, automation, continuous improvement, customer health scoring
- Typical Stage: 500+ customers
- Indicator: "We prevent issues before they become tickets"

**When to Apply**:
- Assessment planning (where are they now?)
- Roadmap development (where should they go next?)
- Goal setting (what's realistic for their stage?)

**Key Insight**: Can't skip levels - must build foundation before optimization

### Knowledge Base ROI Framework
**Purpose**: Justify investment in self-service resources

**Input Metrics**:
- Average ticket resolution cost: $25-50 (support specialist time)
- Monthly ticket volume: [X tickets]
- Estimated KB deflection rate: 20-40% (industry standard)

**ROI Calculation**:
```
Monthly Savings = (Ticket Volume × Deflection Rate) × Cost per Ticket
Annual Savings = Monthly Savings × 12
KB Investment = $10-20K (content creation, tool, maintenance)
Payback Period = Investment ÷ Monthly Savings
```

**Example**:
- 500 tickets/month × 30% deflection × $35/ticket = $5,250/month saved
- Annual savings: $63,000
- Investment: $15,000
- Payback: 2.9 months

**When to Apply**:
- Client questions KB investment value
- Building business case for self-service
- Prioritizing support improvements

## Diagnostic Frameworks

### Support Problem Diagnosis Framework
**Purpose**: Systematically identify root cause of support issues

**The 5 Why's Applied to Support**:

**Problem**: CSAT declining

1. **Why?** → Customers reporting slow responses
2. **Why?** → Resolution times increased from 1 day to 4 days
3. **Why?** → Complex tickets getting stuck
4. **Why?** → No clear escalation path to engineering
5. **Why?** → All-hands support model, unclear ownership

**Root Cause**: Organizational structure (no escalation path), not individual performance

**Framework Steps**:
1. Identify the symptom (CSAT decline)
2. Ask "why" repeatedly until you hit systemic cause
3. Avoid stopping at individual/effort level
4. Look for structural/process root causes
5. Recommend systemic fixes

**When to Apply**: When metrics declining, customers complaining, team struggling

### [Field] Investment Prioritization Framework
**Purpose**: Allocate limited resources to highest-impact improvements

**Prioritization Matrix**:
```
             High Impact │ Low Impact
             ─────────────┼─────────────
High Effort  │  Evaluate  │  Avoid
             │    Later   │
             ─────────────┼─────────────
Low Effort   │    Do      │  Maybe
             │   First!   │
```

**Scoring**:
- **Impact**: Customer satisfaction improvement potential (High/Med/Low)
- **Effort**: Implementation time and complexity (High/Med/Low)

**Examples**:
- **Do First** (High Impact, Low Effort): Escalation process, email templates
- **Evaluate Later** (High Impact, High Effort): Full knowledge base, customer success program
- **Maybe** (Low Impact, Low Effort): Ticket form improvements, signature updates
- **Avoid** (Low Impact, High Effort): Custom tool integrations, premature automation

**When to Apply**:
- Roadmap planning
- Resource allocation decisions
- Balancing quick wins with strategic initiatives
