# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> Last updated: 2026-01-30
> Source: /setup configuration

## Overview

Noorha Transport serves four primary customer segments: corporate clients requiring reliable transportation for employees and visitors (especially those with Aramco compliance needs), international business travelers needing seamless airport-to-hotel or cross-border transport, hospitality partners providing guest transportation, and individual travelers (expats and locals) needing professional taxi service in the Bahrain-Dammam corridor. Our ideal customers value reliability, professionalism, and proper documentation over the lowest price.

---

## Customer Segments

### Segment 1: Corporate Clients (Highest Value)

**Profile**: Companies with Aramco projects or regular business travel needs requiring reliable, compliant transportation for employees visiting sites or traveling between KSA and Bahrain.

**Examples**:
- Maxtube
- AlBawani Capital
- Maguin

**% of Business**: ~40%
**Value**: High (recurring revenue, premium pricing, referrals)

**Demographics**:
- **Industry**: Oil & gas, construction, engineering, consulting
- **Company Size**: 20-500 employees (mid-market to enterprise)
- **Geography**: Based in or serving Eastern Province (Dammam, Khobar, Dhahran)
- **Aramco involvement**: Contractors or service providers to Saudi Aramco

**Needs**:
- Full compliance documentation (Journey Management Plans, Vehicle Fitness Checklists)
- GPS tracking for safety monitoring
- Reliable, on-time service for site visits and meetings
- Professional drivers familiar with Aramco protocols
- Proper VAT invoicing for corporate accounting
- Predictable, transparent pricing

**Buying Behavior**:
- Decision makers: Operations Manager, HR Manager, Office Manager
- Buying trigger: New Aramco project, recurring site visits needed
- Decision criteria: Compliance first, then reliability, then price
- Typical cycle: Initial trial trip, then monthly account setup
- Payment: Bank transfer with monthly invoicing

**How to Win Them**:
- Lead with compliance capabilities
- Provide sample documentation upfront
- Offer dedicated account management
- Ensure consistent driver and vehicle assignment

**Demographics**:

- Reports to: Country Manager, Regional Director
- Company size: Mid-market companies with Aramco contracts
- Experience: 5-15 years in operations/admin

**Psychographics**:
- Goals: Ensure employee safety, meet Aramco compliance, manage budgets
- Challenges: Finding reliable transportation that meets audit requirements
- Motivations: Avoid compliance issues, ensure employee satisfaction
- Fears: Failed audit, employee safety incident, budget overruns

**Day in the Life**:
- Spends time on: Coordinating logistics, managing vendors, tracking expenses
- Measured by: Compliance adherence, employee satisfaction, cost control
- Frustrated by: Unreliable vendors, missing documentation, last-minute cancellations

**How They Buy**:
- Research: Word-of-mouth from other operations managers, Google search
- Influences: Employee feedback, audit requirements, budget approval
- Decision: Trial trip to test service, then evaluate documentation quality
- Timeline: 1-2 week evaluation period

## Qualifying Criteria

### Must-Haves (Deal Breakers)
- [ ] Need for transportation in Eastern Province or KSA-Bahrain corridor
- [ ] Value reliability and professionalism (not just cheapest price)
- [ ] Can pay via bank transfer or cash (have payment capability)

### Strong Indicators (Corporate Segment)
- [ ] Aramco contractor or service provider
- [ ] Regular site visits or employee travel needs
- [ ] Current vendor is unreliable or lacks documentation
- [ ] Recently won new Aramco project

### Strong Indicators (International Business Traveler)
- [ ] Frequent travel to Eastern Province
- [ ] Senior role (values premium service)
- [ ] Company books travel for them
- [ ] Cross-border travel to Bahrain needed

### Red Flags
- [ ] Primarily price-focused (looking for absolute cheapest option)
- [ ] One-time leisure trip with no future business potential
- [ ] Unrealistic expectations (e.g., expecting luxury service at economy pricing)
- [ ] Payment issues or history of non-payment

## Anti-ICP (Who to Avoid)

### Not a Good Fit
- Large-scale public transportation needs (we focus on premium, individualized service)
- Budget leisure travelers seeking rock-bottom prices
- Same-day moving or cargo services (outside our service scope)
- Clients needing vehicles without drivers (we don't do rental-only)

### Why They Don't Work
- **Budget leisure travelers**: Our premium service and compliance focus means higher pricing than informal taxi services. Budget travelers will churn to cheaper options.
- **Large shuttle contracts**: We excel at relationship-based service, not high-volume shuttle operations.
- **Non-payment risk**: Individual leisure travelers have higher non-payment risk than corporate accounts with invoicing.
