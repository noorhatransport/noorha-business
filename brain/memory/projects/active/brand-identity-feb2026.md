## Objective

Define Noorha Transport's visual brand identity—logo, color palette, typography, and basic brand guidelines—to ensure consistent, professional presentation across all digital touchpoints (website, GMB, social media, marketing materials).

**Success = Brand identity that**:
- Communicates professionalism, reliability, and premium service
- Works across all digital and print applications
- Differentiates from competitors
- Appeals to target customers (corporate clients, business travelers)
- Can be implemented immediately on website and GMB

---

## Background

### Why Brand Identity First

**Current State**:
Noorha Transport may have informal or inconsistent branding. For digital growth, we need:
- Logo for website, GMB profile, marketing materials
- Color palette for website design and visual consistency
- Typography standards for professional presentation
- Basic guidelines so all materials look cohesive

**Dependencies**:
- **Website Revamp** needs logo and colors (Week 1)
- **GMB Profile** needs logo for profile photo (Week 1)
- **All future marketing** needs consistent brand identity

This project BLOCKS everything else, so it must complete early February.

### Approach: Lean & Pragmatic

We're not building a 100-page brand book. We need:
1. **Logo** (primary mark that represents Noorha)
2. **Color palette** (3-5 colors for website and materials)
3. **Typography** (2-3 fonts for headings, body, accents)
4. **Basic guidelines** (logo usage, color combos, do's and don'ts)

Get to "good enough to launch" quickly, refine over time as needed.

## Success Criteria

### Logo Design
- [ ] **Primary logo**: Main mark representing Noorha Transport
- [ ] **Variations**: Horizontal, stacked, icon-only versions
- [ ] **File formats**: Vector (SVG, AI) and raster (PNG, JPG) formats
- [ ] **Color versions**: Full color, white, black, grayscale
- [ ] **Clear space rules**: Minimum spacing around logo
- [ ] **Minimum size**: Smallest size logo can be used (legibility)

### Logo Should Convey
- **Professionalism**: Not amateur or cheap-looking
- **Reliability**: Trustworthy, established (20+ years)
- **Premium quality**: Matches fleet (Denali, Escalade)
- **Transportation**: Clear connection to service
- **Saudi/regional context**: If appropriate (not required)

### Color Palette
- [ ] **Primary color**: Main brand color (1 color)
- [ ] **Secondary color(s)**: Supporting colors (1-2 colors)
- [ ] **Accent color**: For CTAs and highlights (1 color)
- [ ] **Neutral colors**: Grays, blacks, whites for text and backgrounds
- [ ] **Hex codes provided**: For digital use
- [ ] **RGB/CMYK values**: For print (if needed)

### Color Psychology Considerations
- **Blues**: Trust, professionalism, reliability (common in corporate)
- **Blacks/Grays**: Premium, sophisticated, VIP service
- **Golds/Coppers**: Luxury, high-end (use sparingly)
- **Greens**: Not typical for transportation (skip)
- **Reds**: Energy, urgency (could work for "fast" positioning)

### Typography
- [ ] **Heading font**: Strong, readable font for titles (1 font)
- [ ] **Body font**: Clean, legible font for paragraphs (1 font)
- [ ] **Accent font** (optional): For special use cases
- [ ] **Font weights specified**: Regular, bold, etc.
- [ ] **Font sources**: Google Fonts (free) or licensed fonts

### Brand Guidelines Document
- [ ] **1-2 page brand guide PDF** covering:
  - Logo usage (correct and incorrect)
  - Color palette (when to use each)
  - Typography (headings, body, sizes)
  - Example applications (website header, business card, etc.)
  - Do's and Don'ts

## Brand Personality Framework

### Noorha Transport Brand Attributes

**Primary Attributes** (Must convey):
1. **Professional**: Not casual or amateur, business-grade service
2. **Reliable**: 20+ years proves we deliver consistently
3. **Premium**: Fleet quality (Denali, Escalade) matches brand

**Secondary Attributes** (Nice to convey):
4. **Experienced**: Deep market knowledge, proven track record
5. **Systematic**: Organized, documented, professional processes
6. **Safe**: Aramco compliance, GPS tracking, trained drivers

**Avoid**:
- Budget/cheap positioning (we're premium)
- Overly playful or casual (corporate clients need professionalism)
- Generic "every transportation company" look (we have differentiators)

### Target Audience Perception

**Corporate clients should think**:
"This looks like a company I can trust with my executives and compliance needs."

**Business travelers should think**:
"This looks like a premium service that will be reliable and comfortable."

**Comparison**:
- More professional than Uber/Careem (app-based variable quality)
- As premium as high-end car services
- More modern than old-school taxi companies

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Design-by-Committee / Endless Revisions
**Impact**: Delays website and GMB projects
**Likelihood**: Medium (design feedback can be subjective)

**Mitigation**:
- **Set revision limit**: Max 2 rounds of feedback
- **Focus on "good enough to launch"**: Can refine later
- **Time-box decision**: Must decide by Feb 7 (Week 1 deadline)
- **Zulfan has final say**: Single decision-maker prevents deadlock

### Risk 2: Logo Doesn't Work at Small Sizes
**Impact**: Poor legibility on mobile, GMB, favicons
**Likelihood**: Low-Medium

**Mitigation**:
- Test logo at multiple sizes during design
- Ensure icon version works at 32x32 pixels (favicon size)
- Muhammad experienced with digital design, knows size requirements

### Risk 3: Colors Don't Work on Website
**Impact**: Redesign needed, delays
**Likelihood**: Low

**Mitigation**:
- Muhammad tests color palette on sample website mockup
- Checks contrast ratios for accessibility
- Ensures colors work for CTAs, backgrounds, text

### Risk 4: Brand Identity Feels Generic
**Impact**: Doesn't differentiate from competitors
**Likelihood**: Low-Medium

**Mitigation**:
- Research competitors first (avoid similar looks)
- Incorporate unique elements (Aramco focus, premium fleet, cross-border)
- Zulfan feedback ensures it feels like "Noorha"
