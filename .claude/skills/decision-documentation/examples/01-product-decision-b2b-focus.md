# Decision: Implement Tiered Support Model for SaaS Client

**Date:** 2025-11-20
**Context:** CX Strategy
**Participants:** Ash, Client Leadership Team
**Status:** Active (Review: 2025-04-01)

---

## Decision Made

Client will implement a tiered support model (L1/L2/L3) rather than continuing with all-hands support approach.

---

## Why This Decision?

### Problem
Current all-hands support model doesn't scale beyond 200 customers. Engineers spending 40% of time on basic support questions.

### Options Considered
1. **Continue All-Hands Support**
   - Pros: High touch, engineers close to customers
   - Cons: Expensive, not scalable, engineer burnout

2. **Tiered Support Model** ✅ **CHOSEN**
   - Pros: Scalable, clear career path, protects engineering time
   - Cons: Setup cost, training overhead, potential knowledge gaps

3. **Outsourced Support**
   - Pros: Lower cost, 24/7 coverage possible
   - Cons: Loss of product knowledge, cultural misalignment

### Rationale
- L1 handles 60-70% of tickets (documentation, basic troubleshooting)
- L2 handles 25-30% (technical issues, bug triage)
- L3 (engineering) handles <10% (complex bugs, architecture questions)
- Protects engineering capacity while maintaining quality
- Creates career progression path for support team

---

## Trade-offs Accepted

**Not doing (for now):**
- Full 24/7 support coverage
- Outsourced support team
- Dedicated customer success team (keeping combined CS/Support)

**Gaining:**
- Engineering time back for product development
- Clear escalation paths
- Specialized support expertise
- Scalable support operations

---

## Implementation Impact

**Affects:**
- Hiring: Need 2 L1 support specialists
- Process: Create escalation procedures and SLAs
- Tools: Implement ticket routing in support platform
- Knowledge: Expand documentation and self-service resources

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Create tiering criteria and escalation matrix
- [ ] Document L1/L2 scope and handoff procedures
- [ ] Hire and train two L1 support specialists
- [ ] Update support workflows in company brain

---

## Review Criteria

**Revisit if:**
- L1 ticket resolution rate falls below 60%
- Customer satisfaction scores drop below 4.0/5.0
- Engineering time on support exceeds 15%

**Success metrics:**
- L1 resolves 65%+ of tickets
- Average first response time under 2 hours
- Engineering time on support reduced to <10%
- Customer satisfaction maintains 4.2+/5.0

**Scheduled review:** April 1, 2025

---

## Related Decisions
- Influences: Hiring plan, support tool selection, knowledge base expansion
- Related to: Customer success team structure

---

## Notes
This is a transitional step. As customer base grows beyond 500, may need dedicated L3 support engineer separate from product engineering team.
